10,000 Ruby developers helping each other
make their apps faster. Since 2016.

The Rails Performance Slack is a community of about 10,000 Ruby developers who talk about one thing: making Ruby and Rails applications faster. Buy any of my three books and you'll get an invite.

Stuck on a performance problem? Someone in here has already solved it.

Performance work is lonely. You're usually the only person at your company staring at the APM dashboard, wondering if a 300 millisecond average response time is good or bad, or why the memory graph only ever goes up. Your coworkers can't help. Stack Overflow answers are ten years old. The blog post you found doesn't quite match your situation.

In the Rails Performance Slack, the person answering your question has usually seen your exact problem before - in production, at scale, more than once. Database queries, memory bloat, Sidekiq queues, Puma tuning, frontend performance, capacity planning: whatever flavor of slow you're dealing with, somebody in there has dealt with it too.

I'm in there as well, reading and answering questions, like I have been since 2016.

Started as a book club, became the watercooler

The Slack started in 2016 as an add-on to The Complete Guide to Rails Performance - buy the book, get access to the channel. Since then, I've added every reader of every book I've written, plus all of my consulting clients. Ten years later, it's about 10,000 people.

When readers and clients tell me what they got out of working with me, the Slack comes up over and over. People have told me it's one of the most valuable things I make. It even got a shout-out on stage at Blue Ridge Ruby this year. I've watched members debug each other's memory leaks, review each other's database schemas, and talk each other out of expensive infrastructure mistakes.
